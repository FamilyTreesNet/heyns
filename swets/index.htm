John A. Swets

John A. Swets is chief scientist (emeritus) after 40 years with BBN Technologies in Cambridge MA. He is senior research associate in radiology at the Brigham and Women's Hospital and lecturer on health care policy, Harvard Medical School. Earlier, he was assistant and associate professor of psychology at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. He was awarded the Warren Medal of the Society of Experimental Psychologists and the Distinguished Scientific Contribution Award of the American Psychological Association. He is past chair of the Commission on Behavioral and Social Sciences and Education of the National Research Council. Swets is a member of the National Academy of Sciences and current chair of its Psychology Section. He received a Ph.D. in Psychology from The University of Michigan in 1950, based on research with fellow graduate student Wilson P. Tanner, Jr. He co-authored with David M. Green in 1966, Signal detection theory and psychophysics. (Biographic Sketch)
